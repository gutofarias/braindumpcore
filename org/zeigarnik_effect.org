#+title: Zeigarnik Effect

Simply put, the Zeigarnik effect is the observation that /interrupted
tasks are better remembered than completed ones/. This has
ramifications for [[id:563dcf37-472a-4203-b037-5a1635084ae9][productivity]].

An example of applying the Zeigarnik Effect would be to stop coding
halfway while having a good streak, and come back another day.
